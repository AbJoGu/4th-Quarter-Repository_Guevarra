Lesson 1: Newton's Laws of Motion

____________________________________________________________________________________________________________


Newton's Laws are 3 fundamental rules the help us predict the motion of objects. They are the basis for

classical mechanics, and they have been used to explain a wide range of phenomena, from the motion of planets

to the behavior of subatomic particles. These are the three laws of motion:

1. An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by an external force.

2. The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass.

3. For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.



To further understand the first law, we should consider Galileo's thought experiment on intertia. He imagined a

ball rolling down a slope and rolling back up the other side.



He reasoned that if there was no friction, the ball would continue to roll back and forth indefinitely. And

since the ball always reaches the same height as it started (again, disregarding fricttion), when the

second slope is flattened, the ball will never reach the same height as it started, and will continue to

roll forever.
