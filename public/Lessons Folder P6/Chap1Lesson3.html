Lesson 3: Kinematics

____________________________________________________________________________________________________________


Kinematics is the branch of physics that describes the motion of objects without considering the forces that

cause the motion. It focuses on the concepts of displacement, velocity, and acceleration. The most basic

equation of kinematics is:


v = u + at


where v is the final velocity, u is the initial velocity, a is acceleration, and t is time.

Kinematics can be used to analyze the motion of objects in one, two, or three dimensions.
