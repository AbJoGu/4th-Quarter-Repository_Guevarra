Chapter Summary

____________________________________________________________________________________________________________


In this chapter, we have covered the basics of physics, including the difference between scalar and vector

quantities, and the fundamentals of kinematics. We have learned that physics is the study of matter, energy,

and their interactions, and that it seeks to understand the fundamental principles that govern the universe.

We have also explored how kinematics describes the motion of objects without considering the forces that

cause the motion, and we have derived important equations to analyze motion in one, two, or three dimensions.


Table of Scalar and Vector Quantities:

Scalar Quantities Vector Quantities
Mass Displacement
Temperature Velocity
Energy Acceleration

List of Important Kinematic Equations:

1. v = u + at

2. d = ut + 1/2 at^2

3. v^2 = u^2 + 2ad


where v is the final velocity, u is the initial velocity, a is acceleration, t is time, and d is displacement.
